Help

Getting started:

py-inventory is a simple app that allows for tracking of household objects by vendor and generates shopping lists accordingly. To use py-inventory, the first thing you should do is configure at least one vendor. You must have a vendor before you can configure and track individual items.

The best way to run this app in my opinion is to host it in the cloud (I use Heroku, and this project is configured to be pushed to there with no further setup), and then configure a raspberry pi with a touchscreen and a barcode scanner to sit on the remove product screen. I installed the screen above my kitchen trash can, and when I throw an item away, I scan it first. The scanner sends a newline at the end of the code, so I don't have to touch the screen at all to remove product in quantities of one. To scan product in, just pust the "plus" on the header and scan the same way.

If the app is cloud hosted, you can view your shopping lists on your smartphone while you are in the store (or send an email with needed items). The shopping lists are done in dismissable div tags, so you can press the list item to remove it as you shop. These are just dismissable divs, not two way data binding, so a refresh will restore the entire list. Personally I just use a desktop bookmark for the vendors page. From there I can select the vendor list that I need based on the store that I am in.

Another cool thing I like to do is use a barcode keyboard on my Android (not sure if they have one for iOS). The barcode keyboard allows you to populate forms with scanned barcodes, so it is a nice substitute for the raspberry pi and barcode scanner (though to be honest the keyboard is a lot more cumbersome than the simplicity of the raspberry pi setup)

Vendors:

To configure a vendor, login to the admin panel of py-inventory and click the "add" button next to "vendor". All you need to do from there is configure a vendor name and email where you want to send shopping lists. This could be your email, or it could be the email of the vendor if they offer delivery or shopping services. After you have at least one vendor, you can configure the items that come from that particular vendor.

Items:

Item objects have a name, description, cost, vendor, quantity, use quantity, order point, max quantity. and barcode. All item movement can be controlled by a barcode scanner, or a barcode keyboard on your smartphone. This will allow you to scan items as you throw them away to automatically generate a list of needed items when the inventory level falls below the order point.

Emails:

If you want to configure emails to yourself or a vendor, configure the email settings near the top of the settings.py folder in the main project folder. Once configured, You can send lists of needed items by vendor.
